Reflexive Verbs

In Spanish, there is a group of verbs that are conjugated along with a set of prounouns. These verbs are called reflexive verbs. They have many uses, which you can find below.

Change in Meaning

Verbs can change meaning when used reflexively and when not.

aburrir to bore aburrirse to get bored
acostar to put to bed acostarse to go to bed
bañar to bathe bañarse to bathe oneself

Reciprocal Actions

Sometimes the reflexive pronouns are used to express a reciprocal action (to each other, to one another)

ex: We kiss each other.

Nos besamos.

ex: They hugged one another.

Se abrazaron.

Impersonal Se

Se can be used impersonally to make a passive sentence.

ex: Paella is eaten in Spain.

Se come paella en España.

ex: They speak Spanish and English in this store.

Se hablan español e inglés en esa teinda.

Conjugation

To conjugate reflexive verbs, you simply conjugate the original verb in any tense, and add the appropriate reflexive pronoun in front of the conjugated verb. To find a list of the reflexive pronouns, click here.

ex: I shower. I showered. I will shower.

Me ducho. Me duché. Me ducharé.

ex: You shower. You showered. You will shower.

Te duches. Te duchiste. Te ducharás.

ex: He showers. He showered. He will shower.

Se duche. Se duchó. Se duchará.
