Use of Conditional Perfect

The conditional perfect is used as translated: would have ____ed. The action does not actually occur, but it would have if certain conditions had been met.¹ Haber means “to have”. Conditional perfect is when you conjugate haber in the conditional, making it “would have”. Adding the past participle is the “___ed”.

Examples

I would have eaten in the restaurant already.

Habría comido en el restaurante ya.

She would have been running for an hour already.

Habría estado corriendo por una hora ya.

I would have completed the project before going out.

Habría cumplido el proyecto antes de salir.

They would have gone to the market.

Habrían ido al mercado.

Conjecture

This tense is also used to express probability in a past time² using the word had.

I wondered if she had left.

¿Habría salido ella?

They probably had gone on vaction.

Habrían ido de vacaciones.
