Use of Future Perfect

The future perfect is used as translated: will have ____ed. The action will have been completed in the future.¹ Haber means “to have”. Future perfect is when you conjugate haber in the future, making it “will have”. Adding the past participle is the “___ed”. It can also be used in conjecture.

Examples

I will have eaten in the restaurant already.

Habré comido en el restaurante ya.

She will have been running for an hour already.

Habrá estado corriendo por una hora ya.

I will have completed the project by tomorrow.

Habré cumplido el proyecto por mañana.

They will have gone to the market by then.

Habrán ido al mercado por luego.

Conjecture

This tense is also used to express probability in a past time ² using the words have or has.

I wonder if she has left.

¿Habrá salido ella?

They must have gone on vaction.

Habrán ido de vacaciones.
