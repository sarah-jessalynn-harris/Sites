Past Subjunctive Conjugation

When conjugating verbs, there are rules for each tense. There are certain forms for each tense and for each subject, based off of the original infinitive. There are regular rules; however, every tense has its irregularities. Below you can find out how to conjugate the past subjunctive in Spanish.

Regular Conjugation

Take the third person plural (ellos form) of the preterite and drop –aron or -ieron. Add the appropriate endings. There are two sets of endings that are acceptable for use in the past subjunctive. They are both shown below.

-ar
-ara -áramos
-aras -arais
-ara -aran
-er
-iera -iéramos
-ieras -ierais
-iera -ieran
-ir
-iera -iéramos
-ieras -ierais
-iera -ieran
nadar
nadara nadáramos
nadaras nadarais
nadara nadaran
vender
vendiera vendiéramos
vendieras venderais
vendiera vendieran
vivir
viviera viviéramos
vivieras vivierais
viviera vivieran

Irregular Conjugation

If you use the third person plural (ellos form) of the preterite to form the past subjunctive, then all the irregularities should be addressed already.
