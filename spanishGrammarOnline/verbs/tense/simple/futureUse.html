Use of Future Tense

Verbs are used to describe actions. Tense describes when these actions take place. The future tense of the indicative mood generally refers to the future: what is going to happen. It can also have other contexts, as can be seen below.

Events in the Present

The present tense, as it's name implies, is used to talk about events taking place in the future: things that will happen.

One day, I will travel to England.

Algún día, viajaré a Inglaterra.

Future of Probability/ Present Conjecture

The future tense is used to make assumptions and guesses about things occuring in the present.

ex: Where can Ana be?

¿Dónde estará Ana?

ex: She must be at home.

Estará en casa.

ex: I wonder when they are coming.

¿Cuándo vendrán ellas?

ex: She’ll probably arrive at six.

Llegará a las seis.

ex: I wonder what time it is.

¿Qué hora será?

ex: It may be six already.

Ya será las seis.

Commands

Sometimes, the future tense is used as a command. It most likely takes on a very strong meaning, so I advise that this form should be used with caution.

ex: You will pass me the salt.

Me pasarás la sal.

ex: You will give me the apple that's next to you.

Me dará la manzana que está al lado de Ud.
