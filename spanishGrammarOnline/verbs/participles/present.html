Uses of the Present Participle

Present participles, also known as gerunds, are “-ing” words in English. They are a type of verb form that do not use subjects. They have many uses which you can explore below.

As an Adverb

The gerund can be used as an adverb when two things are happening at the same time ¹

ex: She left the house crying.

Ella salió de la casa llorando.

Explanitory Expression

The gerund can be used as an explanatory expression, subordinate to another verb ²

ex: Being in Barcelona, we visited the Sagrada Famalia.

Estando en Barcelona, visitemos la Sagrada Familia.

Descriptive Expression

The gerund can be used as a descriptive expression, as seen on posters, paintings, or photos ³

ex: She left the house crying.

Ella salió de la casa llorando.

Present Progressive

The gerund can be used to form progressive tenses (see auxiliary verbs and the present progressive).

With Pronouns

Object pronouns can be attached to gerunds, just be sure to add accents if the stressed syllable changes due to the addition of another syllable, following the standard rules of accentuation. To learn more about pronouns, visit this page.

ex: I am talking to her.

Estoy hablándole.

OR

Le estoy hablando.

Regular Forms

Below you can find out how to form present participles normally. There are some irregularities, and you can find them further down the page.

Forming Regular Gerunds

To form a gerund, you drop the ending of the infinitive and add the apropriate endings. For -ar verbs, drop the -ar and add -ando. For -er and -ir verbs, drop the -er or -ir and add -iendo.

-ar
-ando
nadar
nadando
andar
andando
cocinar
cocinando
estudiar
estudiando
-er
-iendo
vender
vendiendo
perder
perdiendo
coser
cosiendo
correr
corriendo
-ir
-iendo
vivir
viviendo
recibir
recibiendo
descubrir
descubriendo
salir
saliendo

Irregular Forms

Present participles can be irregular, too. They can have spelling changes or stem changes. You can lean more by looking at the information below.

Spelling Changes

For -er and -ir verbs with a vowel in front of the ending, the i in -iendo becomes y. In other words, you replace -er or -ir with -yendo.

creer
creyendo
caer
cayendo
oír
oyendo
destruir
destruyendo

Stem Changes

For -ir verbs with a stem change of e to i or o to u in the preterite, they will also change in the present participle.

pedir
pidiendo
seguir
siguiendo
morir
muriendo
dormir
durmiendo
