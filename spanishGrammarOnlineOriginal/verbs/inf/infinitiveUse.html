Infinitive Use

With Conjugated Verbs As a Subject With Prepositions As a Command

Use of Infinitives

Verbs are used to describe actions. The infinitive is the original form of a verb, often phrased as “to _____” in English. You conjugate infinitives to give a verb a subject, tense, and mood. There are other times we use infinitives in sentences as well. These instances, their resonings, and examples are listed below.

In Relation to Conjugated Verbs

In Spanish, infinitives are to be placed after a conjugated verb when the subject of both verbs are the same.

ex: I want to go. We should eat.

Quiero ir. Debemos comer.

ex: She can sing. He knows how to cook.

Puede cantar. Sabe cocinar.

As a Subject

In Spanish the infinitive is used, with or without the masculine article “el”, as a subject, predicate, or complement in a sentence where in English, gerunds are used instead. ¹

ex: Walking with you is a favorite pastime of mine.

(El) caminar contigo es un pasatiempo favorito para mí.

ex: Reading is learning.

Leer es aprender.

With Prepositions

In Spanish, infinitives come after prepositions, not gerunds ²

ex: Before walking with you, I was in class.

Antes de caminar contigo, estaba en clase.

ex: In order to reach your goals, you have to work a lot.

Para alcanzar tus metas, tienes que trabajar mucho.

Commands

The infinitive is used as a command on many posters of warning and signs displaying rules or regulations.

ex: No running near the pool.

No correr cerca de la piscina.

ex: Please leave through the red door.

Por favor salir por la puerta roja.
