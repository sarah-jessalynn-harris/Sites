Present Subjunctive Conjugation

When conjugating verbs, there are rules for each tense. There are certain forms for each tense and for each subject, based off of the original infinitive. There are regular rules; however, every tense has its irregularities. Below you can find out how to conjugate the present subjunctive in Spanish.

Regular Conjugation

For conjugating the subjunctive tense regularly, take the present first person singular (yo form) and drop the o. Then add the appropriate ending. Present subjunctive -ar endings are present -er/-ir. Subjunctive -er/-ir endings are present -ar endings. You could say that they are “opposites”.

Irregular Conjugation

Verbs often have irregular forms within each tense. If you use the yo form as the basis for conjugation of the present subjunctive, you will catch most irregularities there. Even so, there are still other irregularities that must be remembered.
