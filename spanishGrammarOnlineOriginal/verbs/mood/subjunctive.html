The Subjunctive Mood

The subjunctive is used to convey a special meaning when there is doubt, uncertainty, opinion, or emotion linked to an action. Actions in this mood are not real, and do not occur, at least they haven't at the time it is being used. You also use this mood when influence plays a part in what is being said. The subjunctive is subjective.

Uses for Subjunctive

Many sentences in Spanish have what are known as “que phrases”, or subordinate/dependent clauses. They occur when the second verb will have a different subject than the first one. These phrases are a second half in the sentence and can be in either subjunctive or indicative. This is determined by the first half of the sentence, which can be either indicative or imperative. If the first half is imperative, the second half can be indicative or subjunctive. If the first half is indicative, then more observations must occur to determine wither the subjunctive will be used or not. Reasons to use the subjunctive if the first half of the sentence is indicative are listed below.

1. Doubt

When the indicative indicates doubt, the following que phrase will use the subjunctive.

ex: I doubt that Pablo is here.

Dudo que Pablo esté aquí.

ex: It is doubtful that he is sick.

Es dudoso que esté enfermo.

2. Disbelief

When the indicative indicates disbelief, the following que phrase will use the subjunctive.

ex: I don’t think that Pablo is here.

No creo que Pablo esté aquí.

ex: She doesn’t believe that he is sick.

No cree que esté enfermo.

3. Uncertainty

When the indicative indicates uncertainty, the following que phrase will use the subjunctive.

ex: I’m not sure that Pablo is here.

No estoy seguro que Pablo esté aquí.

ex: It’s not for certain that he is sick.

No es seguro que esté enfermo.

4. Desire

When the indicative indicates a desire, the following que phrase will use the subjunctive.

ex: I want Pablo to be here.

Quiero que Pablo esté aquí.

ex: She wants him to not be sick.

Desea que no esté enfermo.

5. Emotion

When the indicative indicates emotion, the following que phrase will use the subjunctive.

ex: I regret that Pablo isn’t here.

Siento que Pablo no esté aquí.

ex: You are sad that he is sick.

Te entristece que esté enfermo.

6. Command/request

When the indicative indicates a command or request, the following que phrase will use the subjunctive.

ex: I demand that Pablo be here.

Demando que Pablo esté aquí.

ex: She asks that he be well again.

Le pide que esté bien otra vez.

7. Adverb Clauses

Certain adverb clauses in Spanish require the subjunctive to follow after it. A few are listed below.

a fin de que (so that)

para que (in order that)

a menos que (unless)

antes de que (before)

con tal que (provided that)

en caso de que (in case that)

sin que (without)

aunque (even if)

ex: I will go provided that Pablo is there.

Voy con tal que Pablo esté allí.

ex: He came without my knowing it.

Vino sin que yo lo supiera.

ex: I’ll leave even if Pablo is there.

Saldré aunque Pablo esté allí.

ex: I cook so that she isn’t hungry.

Cocino para que no tenga hambre.

Order of Tenses

Once you know if you need to use the subjunctive in the que phrase, you must determine which tense of subjunctive to use. There are four: present subjunctive, past subjunctive, present perfect subjunctive, and pluperfect subjunctive. The guide below will indicate when each tense is to be used according to the tense of the first conjugated verb.

1. If the first conjugated verb’s tense is present, present perfect, future, or a command, use the present subjunctive or the present perfect subjunctive.

Present subjunctive will be used if the subjunctive action happens in the present or the future.

ex: I tell him to come.

Le digo que venga.

ex: I have told him to come.

Le he dicho que venga.

ex: I will tell him to come.

Le diré que venga.

ex: Tell him to come. (Tell him he will come).

Dile que venga.

Present perfect subjunctive will be used if the subjunctive action happens in the past.

ex: I doubt that he has seen her.

Dudo que la haya visto.

ex: I have doubted that he has seen her.

He dudado que la haya visto.

ex: I will doubt that he has seen her.

Dudaré que la haya visto.

2. If the first conjugated verb’s tense is preterite, imperfect, conditional, or pluperfect, use the past subjunctive or the pluperfect subjunctive.

Past subjunctive

ex: I told him to come.

Le dije que viniera.

ex: I was telling him to come.

Le decía que viniera.

ex: I would tell him to come.

Le diría que viniera.

ex: I had told him to come.

Le había dicho que viniera.

Pluperfect subjunctive

ex: I doubted that he had seen her.

Dudé que la hubiera visto.

ex: I was doubting that he had seen her.

Dudaba que la hubiera visto.

ex: I would doubt that he had seen her.

Dudaría que la hubiera visto.

ex: I had doubted that he had seen her.

Había dudado que la hubeira visto.
