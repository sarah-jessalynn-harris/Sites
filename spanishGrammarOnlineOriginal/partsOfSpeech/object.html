Direct Indirect

Introduction to Object

A noun is a word that identifies a person, place, or thing. In Spanish, nouns have a gender and a number. Gender means masculine and feminine. Number means how many: singular or plural.

Direct

Direct objects answer the question of “whom?” or “what?”. They describe the recipient of the action.

ex: I make cakes.

Hago pasteles.

Often, the direct object is not stated as a noun, but as a pronoun, using direct object pronouns . These pronouns are placed before the first conjugated verb, or attached to infinitives or gerunds (present participles) .

ex: I make them.

Los hago.

ex: I am making them.

Estoy haciéndolos*

Los estoy haciendo.

ex: I want to make them.

Quiero hacerlos.

Los quiero hacer.

* When attaching pronouns, one must add accent to keep the original stressed syllable if the extra syllable will cause the stressed syllable to change

Indirect

Indirect objects answer the question “to whom?” or “to what?”. They describe the recipient of the action.

ex: I talk to Isabel and Ana.

Hablo a Isabel y Ana.

Often, the indirect object is not stated as a noun, but as a pronoun, using indirect object pronouns . These pronouns are placed before the first conjugated verb, or attached to infinitives or gerunds (present participles) . If you do not use a pronoun, you must state the noun behind the preposition "a". This type of "a" phrase can be accompanied by the pronoun as well, usually for clarification.

ex: I talk to them.

Les hablo.

ex: I am talking to them.

Estoy hablándoles*

Les estoy hablando.

ex: I want to talk to them.

Quiero hablarles.

Les quiero hablar.

* When attaching pronouns, one must add accent to keep the original stressed syllable if the extra syllable will cause the stressed syllable to change
