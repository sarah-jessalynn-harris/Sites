Verbs

Verbs are used to describe actions. Verbs have many forms and many reasons for each to be used. You can find specific information regarding the uses of the different forms of verbs by clicking puzzle pieces next to each category.

Infinitives

The infinitive is the original form of a verb, often phrased as “to _____” in English. You conjugate infinitives to give a verb a subject, tense, and mood.

Reflexive Verbs

Reflexive verbs are a special type of verb in Spanish. They are used often and can have special meanings. These verbs are conjugated along with a reflexive pronoun that accompanies it.

Auxiliary Verbs

Auxiliary verbs play a special part in both English and Spanish grammar. They are sometimes known as “helping verbs” because you can’t use them by themselves; they are meant to help the main verb.

Gustar Verbs

In Spanish, there are a group of verbs that behave differently than other verbs that are used quite often. Verbs like gustar do not translate to English exactly, and have a different formula.

Participles

Participles are forms a verb can take. They are not specific to a subject, and they are used in many different sentance structures for different reasons.

Tense

Tenses tell us what time actions occur in. They also cause verbs to be conjugated in different ways. In Spaniah, there are 14 main tenses.

Mood

Mood expresses how one should process that information. There are three moods for Spanish: indicative, subjunctive, and imperative. They all play a special role in conveying meaning to listeners.
