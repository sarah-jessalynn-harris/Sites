Introduction to Mood

While tense expresses when things happen, mood expresses how one should process that information. There are three moods for Spanish: indicative, subjunctive, and imperative. They all play a special role in conveying meaning to listeners, and it is important to know what mood is being used because it affects how verbs are conjugated.

Indicative Mood

The indicative is about information. The goal of speaking in the indicative mood is to indicate what is happening. It is simply relaying information about an action at some point in time.

Subjunctive Mood

The subjunctive is used to convey a special meaning when there is doubt, uncertainty, opinion, or emotion linked to an action.

Imperative Mood

The imperative mood is used to give commands or instructions. Since Spanish has four different ways to say “you”, there are four different ways to form the imperative, based on which “you” is being commanded.
