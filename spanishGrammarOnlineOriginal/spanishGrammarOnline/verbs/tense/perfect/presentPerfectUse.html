Use of Present Perfect

The present perfect is used as translated: have/has ____ed. The action occurs in the past but is still connected to the present.¹ Haber means “to have”. Present perfect is when you conjugate haber in the present, making it “have”. Adding the past participle is the “___ed”.

Examples

I have eaten in this restaurant before.

He comido en este restaurante antes.

She has been running a lot this week.

Ha estado corriendo mucho este semana.

I have completed the project.

He cumplido el proyecto.

They have gone to the market.

Han ido al mercado.
