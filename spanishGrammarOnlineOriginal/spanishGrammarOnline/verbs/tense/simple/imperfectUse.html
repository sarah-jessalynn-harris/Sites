Imperfect Uses

Verbs are used to describe actions. Tense describes when these actions take place. The present tense of the indicative mood generally refers to the present: what is happening right now. It can also have other contexts, as can be seen below.

Describing The Past

The imperfect is used to describe people, places, things, and emotions in the past.

ex: The dogs were happy.

Los perros estaban alegres.

ex: The sky was grey.

El cielo estaba gris.

ex: My jacket was wet.

Mi chaqueta estaba mojada.

ex: She was kind.

Ella era simpática.

Ongoing Past Actions

Actions that have a long duration, often without a clear beginning or end, are expressed with the imperfect tense.

ex: They were walking while it rained.

Caminaban mientras que llovía.

When these actions are interrupted, the interuption will be formed with the preterite tense.

ex: I was cooking when she called me.

Cocinaba cuando me llamó.

Uncountable Past Actions

The imperfect is used to describe actions of habit that occured in the past over and over again. This also includes actions that were completed seemingly an uncountable number of times. Due to this, I believe that the imperfect has a fitting name, as it is imperfect. All actions happen a countable number of times; however, we cannot count them, and they seem almost infinite in our memories. That makes these actions imperfect.

ex: We used to sing on Sundays.

Cantábamos los domingos.

ex:I would visit him in the summer.

Lo visitaba los veranos.

Expressing Time

The imperfect is used to tell time in the past using ser. ¹

ex: SIt was ten o’clock when we left.

Eran las diez cuando salimos.

Simultaneous Past Events

Often, two actions occur at the same time in the past for an unknown duration of time. In these cases, they will be expressed with the imperfect tense in Spanish. They can also be presented as a list. ²

ex: In high school, I would listen to music while I studied.

En la escuela segundaria, escuchaba música mientras estudiaba

ex: In college, I completed projects and did other homework every week.

En la universidad, cumplía proyectos y hacía otra tarea cada semana.

Progressive Verbs

The imperfect is used with progressive verbs like venir and ir (you wouldn’t use a gerund with these verbs). ³

ex: I was going home.

Iba a casa. (NOT Estaba yendo a casa.)

ex: She was returning from Japan.

Volvía de Japón. (NOT Estaba viniendo de Japón.)

Indirect Speech

When someone has said something to you in the past (usually idicated by the preterite tense), the quote you are repeating will be in the imperfect tense in Spanish. ⁴

ex: He told me that he loved me.

Me dijo que me amaba.

ex: We asked if they had the tickets.

Los preguntamos que si tenían los boletos.

Adding Courtesy

When saying certain things in the imperfect, you can show courtesy, giving a sense of a less harsh tone. ⁵

ex: Well, I want to go! Versus: Well, I wanted to go...

¡Pues, quiero ir! Versus: Pues, quería ir...

ex: What do you want? Versus: What would you like?

¿Qué deseas? Versus: ¿Qué deseabas?
