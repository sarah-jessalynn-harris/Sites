Conditional Tense Conjugation

When conjugating verbs, there are rules for each tense. There are certain forms for each tense and for each subject, based off of the original infinitive. There are regular rules; however, every tense has its irregularities. Below you can find out how to conjugate the conditonal tense in Spanish.

Regular Conjugation

This is how you conjugate verbs regularly in the conditional tense. All three types of verbs, -ar, -er, and -ir, have the same endings for the conditional tense. You add these endings to the end of the infinitive to conjugate in the conditional tense.

Irregular Stems

Some verbs have different stems when conjugated in the conditional or future tenses. Instead of attatching endings to the infinitive, you use a stem that is a changed form of the infinitive.

The verbs poder, caber, haber, and saber all drop the e in the infinitive before adding the endings. Querer has a double r. ¹
