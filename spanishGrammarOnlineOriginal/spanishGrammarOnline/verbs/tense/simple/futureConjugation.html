Future Tense Conjugation

When conjugating verbs, there are rules for each tense. There are certain forms for each tense and for each subject, based off of the original infinitive. There are regular rules; however, every tense has its irregularities. Below you can find out how to conjugate the future tense in Spanish.

Regular Conjugation

This is how you conjugate verbs regularly in the future tense. All three types of verbs, -ar, -er, and -ir, have the same endings for the future tense. You add these endings to the end of the infinitive to conjugate in the future tense.

Irregular Conjugation

Verbs often have irregular forms within each tense. Below you can find the different types of irregular verb conjugations for the future tense of the indicative mood.

Irregular Stems

Some verbs have different stems when conjugated in the future or conditional tenses. Instead of attatching endings to the infinitive, you use a stem that is a changed form of the infinitive.

The verbs poder, caber, haber, and saber all drop the e in the infinitive before adding the endings. Querer has a double r. ¹

poder
podré podremos
podrás podréis
podrá podrán
querer
querré querremos
querrás querréis
querrá querrán

The verbs poner, salir, tener, valer, and venir all swap their second vowel for a d in the infinitive before adding the endings. ²

poner
pondré pondremos
pondrás pondréis
pondrá pondrán
tener
tendré tendremos
tendrás tendréis
tendrá tendrán

The verbs decir drops the ec and hacer drops the ce in the infinitive before adding the endings. ³

decir
diré diremos
dirás diréis
dirá dirán
hacer
haré haremos
harás haréis
hará harán
