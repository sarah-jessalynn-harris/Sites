Preterite Uses

Verbs are used to describe actions. Tense describes when these actions take place. The preterite tense of the indicative mood refers to the definite past: actions that are defined in the past. It can also have other contexts, as can be seen below.

Events in the Past

The preterite is used to describe actions that have been completed in the past within a specific, marked period of time ¹

ex: It rained last night.

Llovió anoche.

Beginnings and Endings

The preterite is used to indicate a beginning or ending of an action ²

ex: It began to get hot.

Empezó a hacer calor.

ex: It stopped raining.

Dejó de llover.

Sequence of Events

The preterite is used to list a sequence of events in the past that end before the next event starts, , using terms like primero, “first”, or después, “afterwards” ³

ex: I arrived home, I showered, and I went to bed.

Llegué a casa, me duché, y me acosté.

ex: First they ate dinner, afterwards they did their homework.

Primero cenaron, después hicieron su tarea.

Abruptitude of Past Events

The preterite is used to describe sudden changes or events in the past ⁴

ex: Suddenly, Juan came into the church.

De repente, Juan entró en la iglesia.

ex: She turned red when it happened.

Ella se puso roja cuando ocurrió.

Interrupting the Imperfect

Often, the preterite is used to describe actions that interrupt other actions that were in progress. The actions that are already in progress will be described with the imperfect.

ex: I was running when I fell.

Yo corría cuando me caí.

ex: He fell while he was walking.

Se cayó mientras que caminaba.

Descriptions That Have Changed

Use the preterite for describing characteristics that have changed. ⁵

ex: My sister was ugly when she was young (but not anymore).

Mi hermana fue fea cuando era joven (pero ya no).
