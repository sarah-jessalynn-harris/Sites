Use of Present Tense

Verbs are used to describe actions. Tense describes when these actions take place. The present tense of the indicative mood generally refers to the present: what is happening right now. It can also have other contexts, as can be seen below.

Events in the Present

The present tense, as it's name implies, is used to talk about events taking place in the present.

ex: I am eating a banana (right now).

Como un plátano (ahora mismo).

Generalities

The present tense is also used to talk about things in a general sense.

ex: Walking with you is a favorite pastime of mine.

(El) caminar contigo es un pasatiempo favorito de mí.

ex: Reading is learning.

Leer es aprender.

ex: Marisol eats bananas for breakfast.

Marisol come plátanos para el desayuno.

What One Does

The present tense can be used to talk about what one does, with the word "do" translating in English. However, the word "do" will not appear as its own seperate word in Spanish.

ex: Do you eat bananas?

¿Comes tú plátanos?

ex: Yes, I do eat bananas.

Sí, como plátanos.

ex: No, I do not eat bananas.

No, no como plátanos.

Near Future

The present tense can be used to talk about future events that will occur within a small timeframe.

ex: I am going to the market tomorrow.

Voy al mercado mañana.

ex: She's coming soon.

Ella viene pronto.

Commands

Sometimes, the present tense is used as a command.

ex: Pass me the salt, please.

Me pasas la sal, por favor.

ex: Give me the apple that's next to you.

Me das la manzana que está al lado de ti.
