Preterite Tense Conjugation

When conjugating verbs, there are rules for each tense. There are certain forms for each tense and for each subject, based off of the original infinitive. There are regular rules; however, every tense has its irregularities. Below you can find out how to conjugate the preterite tense in Spanish.

Regular Conjugation

This is how you conjugate verbs regularly in the preterite tense. -Er and -ir verbs have the same endings for the preterite tense. You drop the ending of the infinitive and add the following endings.

Irregular Conjugation

Verbs often have irregular forms within each tense. Below you can find the different types of irregular verb conjugations for the present tense of the indicative mood.

Stem Changing/Boot Verbs

-Ir verbs that are stem changing in the present are also stem changing in the preterite but differently. The changes are only applied to the 3rd person forms (él and ellos). This can be known as the sole of the boot, referring to the shape stem changing verbs create in the present. In the preterite, the change is only in the sole of the boot shape, as shown below.

Furthermore, the categories of stem changing verbs have a slight change. They are listed below.

e to i

pedir
pedí pedimos
pediste pedisteis
pidió pidieron

e to ie becomes e to i

mentir
mentí mentimos
mentiste mentisteis
mintió mintieron

i to y

Along with the stem change, you also add accents to the i’s of the other forms.

huir
huí huímos
huíste huísteis
huyó huyeron

o to ue (-ar, -er, -ir)

dormir
dormí dormimos
dormiste dormisteis
durmió durmieron

Spelling Changing

Some verbs have spelling changes in the preterite to preserve the pronunciation of the verb.

-car

yo form only

the c will change to qu in front of e

(otherwise it would be cé, pronounced with an "s" sound)

buscar
busqué buscamos
buscaste buscasteis
buscó buscaron

-gar

yo form only

the g will change to gu in front of e

(otherwise it would be gé, pronounced with an "j" sound)

vencer
venzo vencemos
vences vencéis
vence vencen

-zar

yo form only

the z will change to c in front of e

(otherwise it would be zé, pronounced with an "th" sound)

escoger
escojo escogemos
escoges escogéis
escoge escogen

Just Irregular

Certain verbs are just irregular preterite. Some are shown below.

reír

Reír is always a tricky verb to conjugate, given its accent. Here is the correct way to conjugate it in the preterite tense.

reír
reí reímos
reíste reísteis
rió rieron

–ñir

For –ir verbs, the third person singular and plural (él and ellos forms) are –ió and –ieron. For –ñir verbs, simply drop the i from those endings because the ñ already has the i sound.

ceñir (e to i)
ceñí ceñimos
ceñiste ceñisteis
ciñó ciñeron

Certain verbs, such as dar and ver are so short that they don’t need accents in the preterite.

dar
di dimos
diste disteis
dio dieron
ver
vi vimos
viste visteis
vio vieron

ser and ir

Ser and ir are irregular in the preterite and share the exact same conjugation.

ser and ir
fui fuimos
fuiste fuisteis
fue fueron
