Gustar Verbs

In Spanish, there is a group of verbs that behave differently than other verbs. Verbs like gustar do not translate to English exactly, and have a different formula.

Structure

When using verbs like gustar, the subject of the sentence in English is the indirect object in Spanish, and the direct object in English becomes the subject in Spanish. If one were to translate the Spanish literally, it would sound passive in English.

gustar: to like

ex: I like cats.

Me gustan los gatos.

“Cats are pleasing to me.”

Prepositions

Because the subject becomes an indirect object, you must use “a” before stating a name or noun in order to specify, but you must use it in conjunction with the indirect object pronoun.

doler: to hurt

ex: Pedro's arms hurt.

A Pedro le duelen los brazos.

“The arms hurt to Pedro”.

Often this “a” phrase is repeated for emphasis, regardless of the indirect object pronoun.

doler: to hurt

ex: Your arms hurt.

A ti te duelen los brazos.

“The arms hurt to you”.

Number

Furthermore, the verb will conjugate singularly or plurally, depending on its subject. As always, after a conjugated verb, an infinitive is placed. The infinitive will cause the main verb to be singular, which in this case, is the gustar-like verb.

importar: to be important

ex: Going to church is important to you.

A ti te importa ir a la iglesia.

“To go to church is important to you”.
