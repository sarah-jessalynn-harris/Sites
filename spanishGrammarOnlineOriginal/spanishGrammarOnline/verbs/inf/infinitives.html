Introduction to Infinitives

Verbs are used to describe actions. The infinitive is the original form of a verb, often phrased as “to _____” in English. You conjugate infinitives to give a verb a subject, tense, and mood.

Infinitives in Spanish

In Spanish, there are three types of infinitives. In English, you know a verb is in its infinitive form because it begins with “to” such as to eat. In Spanish, the ending of an infinitive will be -ar, -er, or -ir. These groups are important to know because you conjugate verbs of each group similarly. To conjugate verbs, according to each group, you drop the infinitive endings and add new ones, based on which tense and mood you’re using, that match the subject.

Infinitive Types

First Conjugation: -ar verbs

ex: pagar – to pay

nadar – to swim

preparar – to prepare

Second Conjugation: -er verbs

ex: creer – to think

beber – to drink

vender – to sell

Third Conjugation: -ir verbs

ex: vivir – to live

decir – to tell, say

pedir– to ask for
