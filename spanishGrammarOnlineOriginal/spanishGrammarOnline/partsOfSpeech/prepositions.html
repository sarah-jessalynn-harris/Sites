Prepositions

Prepositions link nouns to other words in sentences, and indicate position, direction or time. ¹ These words will often appear in your vocabulary sets, and that is the best way to learn them and how to use them, a few at a time. However, there are a few things that prepositions in Spanish will have in common. They are invariable, meaning that they do not change with gender or number.² They are used differently in all languages, so do not assume you can use them in Spanish exactly the way you would in English, as a general rule. ³

Here are a few Spanish Prepositions

a = to

de = of, in, by

debajo de = underneath of

en = at, on, in

encima de = on top of

lejos de = far away from

cerca de = close to
